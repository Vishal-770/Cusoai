LOGIN AND ACCOUNT ACCESS POLICY

---
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS — LOGIN & ACCESS
---

Q: I can't log in. What do I do?
A: First try "Forgot Password" on the login screen. If you receive the reset email, follow the link (valid 15 minutes). If no email arrives, check spam/junk and confirm you're using the exact registered email. If still stuck, contact support@company.com.

Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page or visit company.com/reset-password. Enter your registered email. A reset link is emailed within 2 minutes. The link expires after 15 minutes — request a new one if it expires.

Q: My account is locked. How do I unlock it?
A: Accounts lock for 30 minutes after 5 failed attempts. After 30 minutes you can try again automatically. For manual unlock, contact support with your registered email and a government-issued ID.

Q: I'm not receiving the password reset email.
A: Check spam, junk, and promotions folders. Also verify you're entering the exact email address on the account — including correct domain. Add noreply@company.com to your contacts. If issues persist, contact support to confirm your registered email.

Q: My 2FA code is not working.
A: Ensure your device's clock is set to automatic/network time (TOTP codes are time-sensitive). Try entering the code immediately after it refreshes. If using SMS 2FA, there may be a carrier delay — wait 2 minutes. If still failing, use a backup code.

Q: I lost access to my authenticator app / 2FA device.
A: Use one of your backup codes to log in. If you don't have backup codes, contact support@company.com — full identity verification (government-issued ID) is required to bypass 2FA for security.

Q: I don't have my backup codes and can't access my 2FA.
A: Contact support@company.com with your account email and a government-issued ID photo. Our team cannot bypass 2FA without this identity verification for your protection.

Q: I think someone else has accessed my account.
A: Immediately change your password and go to Account Settings > Security > Active Sessions and revoke all sessions. Then email security@company.com with details of the suspected breach.

Q: The login page isn't loading or shows an error.
A: Try a different browser or an incognito/private window. Clear cookies for company.com. Disable browser extensions. Check your network — try a different Wi-Fi or network.

Q: I can log in on one device but not another.
A: The issue is likely device-specific. On the failing device: clear browser cache and cookies, update your browser or app, and try incognito mode. If using a work device, your company's proxy or firewall may be blocking the login page.

Q: Social login (Google/Apple/Microsoft) isn't working.
A: In your Google/Apple/Microsoft account settings, revoke permissions for our app and then reconnect. Make sure the social account email matches your registered platform email.

Q: How do I enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)?
A: Go to Account Settings > Security > Two-Factor Authentication. Choose your preferred method (authenticator app recommended), scan the QR code, verify a test code, and save. Store your backup codes securely.

Q: I changed my email address but now can't log in.
A: If your email change is pending verification, check the new email inbox for a verification link. If the change was completed, use the new email to log in.

Q: I get "Invalid credentials" even though my password is correct.
A: This can happen if your password was changed on another device. Try resetting your password. Also ensure Caps Lock is off and autofill isn't inserting an outdated password.

SECTION 1: STANDARD LOGIN OVERVIEW
---


Password reset emails are sent within 2 minutes. If not received, check the spam or junk folder.
Accounts are temporarily locked after 5 consecutive failed login attempts for a period of 30 minutes for security reasons.
To unlock an account manually, the customer must contact support with their registered email address and a government-issued ID for identity verification.
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is strongly recommended and can be enabled in Account Settings.
If a customer suspects their account has been compromised, they should immediately change their password and revoke all active sessions from the Security settings page.
For social login issues (Google, Apple), the problem is often related to permissions. The customer should revoke and re-grant permissions in their social account settings.

SECTION 2: DETAILED LOGIN TROUBLESHOOTING
---

If the "Forgot Password" email is not received within 5 minutes, check the spam, junk, and promotions folders in the email client.
Many email providers apply filters that may redirect automated emails; adding noreply@company.com to the contact list helps ensure future delivery.
If no reset email is found after checking all folders, the customer should verify they are entering the exact email address registered on the account.
Email addresses are case-insensitive during login but are stored in their original case; typos in the email field are a common cause of reset failures.
To verify the registered email address without logging in, customers can contact support with their full name, account username, and approximate signup date.
If the password reset email was deleted before the link was clicked, a new reset must be initiated; old reset links cannot be resent individually.
Browser autofill can sometimes populate incorrect or outdated credentials; try manually typing the email and password rather than using autofill.
Cached credentials in the browser's password manager may be out of sync after a password change; update the saved credentials or delete and re-enter them.
Browser extensions (particularly ad blockers and privacy extensions) can interfere with the login form; try logging in using a private/incognito browser window to rule this out.
Disabling browser extensions temporarily and attempting login again is recommended as a first troubleshooting step for persistent login page errors.
Clearing all cookies and site data for company.com in the browser settings resolves most persistent session-related login issues.
If the login page itself fails to load, check your network connection and DNS settings; try accessing the page via a different network.
Content Security Policy (CSP) headers on the login page prevent cross-site scripting; do not attempt to inject scripts via the URL or form fields.
Mobile app login failures may be caused by an outdated app version; update the app from the App Store or Google Play Store and retry.
On iOS, after an app update, stored Keychain credentials may need to be re-entered; this is a normal iOS security behavior.
If login works on one device but not another, the issue is likely device-specific (cache, cookies, app version, or network proxy settings) rather than account-level.

SECTION 3: ACCOUNT LOCKOUT POLICY AND MANAGEMENT
---

Accounts are automatically locked after 5 consecutive failed login attempts within any 10-minute rolling window.
The lockout duration is 30 minutes, after which the user may attempt to log in again without manual intervention.
After a lockout expires, the failed attempt counter is reset; subsequent failed attempts will trigger a new lockout cycle.
The lockout applies to all login methods (password, social login, SSO), not just one method.
Enterprise administrators can configure the account lockout threshold and duration from the Admin Panel under Security Settings.
Minimum configurable lockout threshold for enterprise is 3 attempts; maximum is 10 attempts.
Minimum lockout duration is 5 minutes; enterprise admins can set it up to 8 hours for high-security environments.
An email notification is sent to the registered email address when an account lockout is triggered, informing the user and providing self-service options.
If the customer believes the lockout was caused by unauthorized login attempts rather than their own mistake, they should immediately contact security@company.com.
Manual unlocking by a support agent requires the customer to authenticate their identity via the registered email address.
In cases where both the account and the registered email are compromised, enhanced identity verification (government-issued ID) is required.
Enterprise administrators can manually unlock a user's account from the Admin Panel > User Management > [User] > Unlock Account.
A forced password reset can be combined with manual unlock by the administrator to require the user to set a new password on next login.
All lockout events, including timestamp, IP address, and device information, are recorded in the security audit log.
Repeated lockout patterns targeting specific accounts are flagged by our security system as potential brute force attacks and trigger enhanced monitoring.
If a brute force attack against an account is confirmed, the originating IP addresses are blocked and the account is placed under security watch.

SECTION 4: PASSWORD RESET DETAILED PROCEDURES
---

The password reset process begins by clicking "Forgot Password" on the login screen or navigating to company.com/reset-password.
The customer enters their registered email address. If the email exists in the system, a reset link is sent; if not, no notification is sent to prevent email enumeration.
The reset email contains a unique single-use link that is cryptographically signed and bound to the requesting user's IP range.
The reset link expires after 15 minutes from the time the email was sent.
If the reset link has expired, the customer must initiate a new password reset request; there is no mechanism to extend existing links.
The new password must meet current password strength requirements: minimum 12 characters, mixed case, numbers, and symbols.
The new password cannot be the same as the current password or any of the last 10 passwords used on the account.
After a successful password reset, all existing sessions across all devices are immediately invalidated for security.
A confirmation email is sent to the registered email address immediately after a password reset is completed successfully.
If the customer receives a password reset confirmation email but did not initiate the reset, they should immediately contact security@company.com.
Clicking the "Click here if you did not request this reset" link in the confirmation email will lock the account until further verification.
Support agents can trigger a forced password reset for an account but cannot set the new password directly; the reset is always completed by the account holder via email.
Password reset attempts are rate-limited per email address; more than 5 reset requests within 1 hour will be throttled and some requests may be delayed.
SMS-based password reset is available as a secondary option when a verified phone number is on the account; a 6-digit OTP is sent.
SMS OTPs for password reset expire after 10 minutes and can only be used once.
If both email and phone are inaccessible, the full account recovery process with identity verification must be followed.

SECTION 5: TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION SETUP AND USAGE
---

Two-Factor Authentication strengthens account login security by requiring a second proof of identity in addition to the password.
The 2FA verification step occurs after a successful password entry; the system prompts for the second factor before completing login.
TOTP-based 2FA (authenticator apps) requires a 6-digit time-based code that changes every 30 seconds.
The TOTP algorithm uses HMAC-SHA1 with a 30-second time step as specified by RFC 6238.
Clock drift on the authenticator device can cause code failures; ensure the device's time is set to automatic/network time synchronization.
If TOTP codes are consistently rejected, try the code immediately when it refreshes to confirm it is not a timing issue.
SMS 2FA sends a numeric code via text message; the code is 6 digits and expires after 10 minutes.
Delivery of SMS codes depends on the recipient's mobile carrier and may be delayed during periods of high network traffic.
Backup codes are 10-character alphanumeric strings that should be treated as equivalent in security to a password.
Each backup code can only be used once; once used, it is crossed off the list.
If fewer than 3 backup codes remain, the system will prompt the user to generate a fresh set of 10.
Generating new backup codes invalidates all previous backup codes immediately.
In situations where neither the primary 2FA method nor backup codes are available, the 2FA recovery process through identity verification is the only option.
Our support team cannot bypass 2FA without completing the full identity verification process, even at an administrator's request. This protects customers against social engineering attacks.
Enterprise SSO with MFA configured at the identity provider level satisfies the 2FA requirement; additional platform-level 2FA is not required when SSO MFA is active.
After 2FA recovery, the customer is required to enroll a new 2FA method within 7 days; accounts without a new 2FA method are flagged.

SECTION 6: SOCIAL LOGIN (GOOGLE, APPLE, MICROSOFT) DETAILS
---

Social login allows customers to authenticate using their existing Google, Apple, or Microsoft account credentials instead of a separate platform password.
When using social login for the first time, a new platform account is created and linked to the social identity provider (IdP).
If a customer already has an account using the same email address, the social login is linked to the existing account after an email verification step.
A platform account can have multiple social login providers linked simultaneously (e.g., both Google and Apple).
Unlinking a social login provider that is the only authentication method on an account requires setting a platform password first.
Social login relies on OAuth 2.0 authorization flows; the platform receives a verified token from the social IdP and never sees or stores the social account's password.
Permission scopes requested from Google login are: email address, name, and profile picture only. No access to Gmail, Drive, or other Google services is requested.
Permission scopes requested from Apple login are: name and email address. Apple's "Hide My Email" relay feature is fully supported.
Permission scopes requested from Microsoft login are: email and basic profile. No access to Microsoft 365 services is requested.
If social login fails with a "Permission Denied" error, the customer should go to their social account's connected apps settings and remove the platform, then attempt social login again to trigger a fresh authorization.
For Google: Manage connected apps at myaccount.google.com/permissions.
For Apple: Manage connected apps at appleid.apple.com > Sign In with Apple.
For Microsoft: Manage connected apps at myaccount.microsoft.com > Privacy > App permissions.
Social login is not affected by platform-level password lockouts; however, if the social IdP locks the social account, social login to the platform will also fail.
Enterprise organizations using SSO cannot use social login; all authentication is routed through the configured SSO identity provider.
Social login accounts are subject to all the same security policies and session management rules as password-based accounts.

SECTION 7: SINGLE SIGN-ON (SSO) FOR ENTERPRISE
---

Enterprise SSO allows organizations to authenticate platform users through their corporate identity provider (IdP) without separate platform credentials.
SSO is configured per organization domain; all users with verified email addresses matching the organization's claimed domain are redirected through SSO.
Supported SSO protocols are SAML 2.0 and OIDC (OpenID Connect).
SAML 2.0 integration requires configuration of the SP Entity ID, ACS URL (Assertion Consumer Service), and the IdP metadata XML.
The SP Entity ID for our platform is: https://app.company.com/auth/saml/metadata.
The ACS URL to configure in your IdP is: https://app.company.com/auth/saml/callback.
OIDC integration requires a client ID, client secret, discovery URL, and confirmation of supported scopes.
Our support team provides a detailed SSO setup guide and sandbox testing environment for initial configuration.
Once SSO is enabled and tested, enabling "Enforce SSO" in the Admin Panel redirects all login attempts through the IdP, disabling direct password login.
Just-in-Time (JIT) provisioning automatically creates platform accounts for users authenticated through SSO who do not yet have an existing platform account.
JIT provisioning assigns new users to a default role defined by the enterprise administrator in the SSO configuration.
Attributes mapped from the IdP can include name, email, department, job title, and custom group memberships.
Group-to-role mapping allows IdP group memberships to automatically assign platform roles (e.g., members of the "Platform Admins" group in the IdP receive the Administrator role on the platform).
SSO session duration is governed by the IdP's token lifetime settings; the platform respects the not-after assertions in SAML tokens.
When a user's IdP session expires, they are redirected to the IdP for re-authentication; they are not required to re-enter platform-specific credentials.
SSO configuration errors (incorrect ACS URL, certificate mismatch) will result in a 400 error; contact enterprise_support@company.com with the SAML response XML for debugging.

SECTION 8: API ACCESS AND AUTHENTICATION
---

API access uses long-lived API tokens that are generated and managed from the Developer Settings section of the account.
API tokens are displayed only once at creation time; if a token is lost, it must be revoked and a new one generated.
API tokens should be stored securely (environment variables, secrets managers) and never committed to version control or embedded in client-side code.
Token scopes define the permissions granted to the API token; available scopes include Read, Write, Admin, and custom resource-level scopes.
Tokens with broad scopes should only be used for trusted server-side integrations; client-facing apps should use OAuth with user-consented scopes.
OAuth 2.0 authorization code flow with PKCE is the recommended authentication method for third-party apps integrating on behalf of users.
The OAuth authorization endpoint is: https://api.company.com/oauth/authorize.
The OAuth token endpoint is: https://api.company.com/oauth/token.
Access tokens issued via OAuth expire after 1 hour; refresh tokens are valid for 30 days and can be used to obtain new access tokens.
Each API request must include the Authorization header with the bearer token: Authorization: Bearer {token}.
API requests without valid authentication return a 401 Unauthorized response.
Requests with valid authentication but insufficient scope return a 403 Forbidden response.
API rate limits are enforced per token; the default limit is 1,000 requests per minute.
Enterprise accounts can request elevated API rate limits through the account manager or by contacting enterprise_support@company.com.
Rate limit status is communicated via X-RateLimit-Limit, X-RateLimit-Remaining, and X-RateLimit-Reset response headers.
Requests that exceed the rate limit receive a 429 Too Many Requests response with a Retry-After header indicating when to retry.
Webhook event subscriptions can be configured in the Developer Settings to push real-time events to external endpoints without polling the API.

SECTION 9: USER ROLES AND PERMISSIONS
---

Platform accounts can have one of the following roles: Owner, Administrator, Manager, Member, Read-Only, and Guest.
The Owner role is assigned to the account creator and has unrestricted access to all features and settings.
Only one Owner can exist per organization; ownership transfer is possible but requires confirmation from the current owner.
Administrators have access to all features except billing plan changes and ownership transfer; they can manage all users and settings.
Managers have access to team-level settings and can add or remove Members from their assigned teams but cannot perform organization-level administration.
Members have access to product features based on their plan tier but cannot manage other users or access administrative settings.
Read-Only users can view all content they have access to but cannot create, edit, or delete any content or settings.
Guests are external users invited to collaborate on specific projects; their access is limited to the projects they are explicitly invited to.
Role assignments are made by Administrators or Owners from the User Management section of the Admin Panel.
Bulk role assignment is available via CSV import in the Admin Panel for organizations managing large user lists.
Custom roles with specific permission combinations can be created on Enterprise plans to satisfy unique organizational access control requirements.
Role changes take effect immediately and existing active sessions reflect the new role without requiring re-login.
All role assignment and change events are logged in the audit trail with the administrator who made the change, the affected user, and the timestamp.
User access can be temporarily suspended without removing the user from the organization; suspended users cannot log in but their data and assignments are preserved.
Offboarding best practice: when a user leaves the organization, suspend the account, transfer ownership of their content, export relevant data, then delete the account.
Deleted user accounts are removed from all team assignments; content they created is reassigned to the organization owner and not deleted automatically.

SECTION 10: SESSION SECURITY AND TRUSTED NETWORK POLICIES
---

Users can configure IP-based access restrictions from Account Settings > Security > Network Restrictions.
IP allowlisting at the account level blocks all login attempts from IP addresses not on the allowlist.
CIDR notation is supported for allowlisting IP ranges (e.g., 192.168.1.0/24 allows all IPs in the 192.168.1.x subnet).
Users can add up to 50 IP addresses or CIDR ranges to their personal IP allowlist.
If a user accidentally locks themselves out by adding an IP restriction while not on the allowed network, support can assist with verification to remove the restriction.
VPN usage is supported; the platform does not block known VPN provider IP ranges by default, though enterprise admins can configure this.
Session cookies are set with Secure, HttpOnly, and SameSite=Strict attributes to prevent interception and cross-site attacks.
The platform enforces HSTS (HTTP Strict Transport Security) with a max-age of 1 year and includeSubDomains to ensure encrypted connections.
All authentication endpoints require HTTPS; attempts to access them over HTTP are automatically redirected.
Certificate pinning is implemented in the native mobile apps to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks in high-risk network environments.
Users in regulated industries (finance, healthcare) can enable "Compliance Mode" in the mobile app, which disables screenshot capture and restricts app access on jailbroken or rooted devices.
Active sessions display the approximate location, device type, and last activity time in Account Settings > Security > Active Sessions.
