REFUND REQUEST POLICY

---
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS — REFUNDS
---

Q: How do I get a refund?
A: Log into your account, go to Billing > Invoice History, click the invoice, and select "Request Refund". Alternatively email billing@company.com with your invoice number. Decisions are made within 2 business days.

Q: How long does a refund take?
A: Credit/debit cards: 5–7 business days. PayPal: instant to PayPal balance. ACH/bank transfer: 3–5 business days. American Express: up to 10 business days.

Q: I want to cancel and get my money back. Am I eligible?
A: You are eligible for a full refund if you cancel within 30 days of your purchase date. Monthly renewals are only refundable if cancelled within 24 hours of the renewal charge.

Q: I was charged twice / double billed. What do I do?
A: Email billing@company.com immediately with both invoice numbers. Duplicate charges are treated as a billing error and refunded in full within 5–7 business days after verification.

Q: I bought the wrong plan by accident. Can I get a refund?
A: Yes. Accidental purchases are eligible for a full refund within 30 days. Select "Accidental Purchase" as the reason when submitting your refund request.

Q: I upgraded my plan and want a refund on the upgrade charge.
A: Upgrade charges are refundable within 30 days of the upgrade date, independent of the original subscription start date.

Q: Why was my refund denied?
A: Common reasons: outside the 30-day window, the item is non-refundable (setup fees, promotional plan, digital download without defect), or the account violated Terms of Service. The denial email will include the specific reason and any available alternatives.

Q: Can I appeal a refund denial?
A: Yes. Reply to the denial email and request a supervisor review. Escalated reviews are completed within 5 business days.

Q: I cancelled my annual plan. Do I get a partial refund?
A: If cancelled within 30 days of purchase, yes — a full refund. If cancelled after 30 days, no partial refund is issued; the account remains active until the annual period ends.

Q: My refund hasn't arrived yet. What should I do?
A: First check that your bank's processing time has passed (up to 10 business days for some cards). If still missing, email billing@company.com with your refund confirmation number.

Q: Can I get a refund as account credit instead of back to my card?
A: Yes. You can request a store credit (applied to your account balance) instead of a card refund. Store credits are applied immediately.

Q: I redeemed a gift card / promo code. Is it refundable?
A: Gift cards and prepaid codes are non-refundable after redemption. Promotional plan purchases specifically marked "non-refundable" at purchase are also excluded.

Q: Can I get a refund for a training session I missed?
A: Training sessions are refundable if cancelled more than 48 hours before the scheduled time. Cancellations within 48 hours receive a 50% refund.

Q: I have a consumer protection right to a refund in my country (e.g., EU). Does your policy honour it?
A: Yes. We honour consumer protection rights under applicable law (including EU consumer rights legislation) regardless of our standard policy. State this clearly in your refund request.

Q: What is not refundable?
A: One-time setup/onboarding fees, custom development work, add-ons after the first billing month (unless purchased in error), domain registration fees, SMS credit top-ups, and items explicitly sold as non-refundable.

SECTION 1: STANDARD REFUND POLICY OVERVIEW
---


Refunds are processed to the original payment method within 5-7 business days after approval.
To initiate a refund, the customer must provide their order ID and the reason for the refund request.
Digital products that have been downloaded are not eligible for a refund unless there is a proven technical defect.
Subscription services can be cancelled at any time, but partial month refunds are not provided.
For defective physical products, we offer a full refund or replacement at no additional cost.
If a refund is approved, the customer will receive a confirmation email with the expected processing timeline.
Bank transfer refunds may take an additional 3-5 business days beyond our standard processing time.

---
SECTION 2: REFUND ELIGIBILITY IN DETAIL
---

All purchases are subject to our 30-day full refund window, which begins at 12:00 AM UTC on the day after the transaction date.
The 30-day refund window applies to new subscriptions, plan upgrades, and annual plan purchases.
For annual plans, the 30-day refund window covers the entire prepaid year; a pro-rated refund is issued for the remaining months if cancelled within 30 days.
Monthly subscription renewals are not eligible for a refund after the renewal date unless the cancellation is initiated within the first 24 hours of a new billing cycle.
Customers who cancel a monthly subscription within 24 hours of renewal may receive a discretionary refund credit equal to one month's subscription value; this is reviewed case by case.
Refund eligibility is determined by the transaction date on the invoice, not the date the subscription feature was first used.
If a customer upgrades from one plan to another, the upgrade charge is eligible for a refund within 30 days of the upgrade date, separate from the original subscription.
Refunds are not available for accounts that have violated the Terms of Service; such refund requests are automatically denied.
Accounts that have previously been refunded more than twice within a 12-month period may be flagged for refund abuse review.
Refund abuse is defined as a pattern of purchasing subscriptions intending to use features and then requesting refunds as a matter of course.
In cases of suspected refund abuse, the Trust and Safety team may deny refund requests and escalate the account for Terms of Service review.
Customers who have received a refund and subsequently sign up for a new trial are subject to additional restrictions; a second trial cannot be initiated within 6 months.

SECTION 3: NON-REFUNDABLE ITEMS AND EXCEPTIONS
---

One-time setup fees, onboarding fees, and professional services fees are non-refundable once the service has been delivered.
Custom development or configuration work delivered under a statement of work is non-refundable upon completion and acceptance.
Add-on purchases (additional storage, extra API calls, premium support packages) are non-refundable after the first billing month unless they were purchased in error.
Promotional or discounted subscription plans that were specifically described as "non-refundable" at the time of purchase are not eligible for refund.
Domain registration or third-party service fees billed through our platform are non-refundable as these are pass-through costs with no return allowance from the registrar.
SMS or communication credit top-ups are subject to a specific credit policy and do not qualify under the standard refund policy.
Hardware or physical goods must be returned in original, unopened packaging within 30 days; a return merchandise authorization (RMA) must be obtained before returning.
Returned hardware items are inspected upon receipt; items with signs of use, damage, or missing components are subject to a restocking fee of up to 25%.
Gift cards and prepaid codes are non-refundable after redemption to the account.
Training session fees are refundable if cancelled more than 48 hours before the scheduled session; cancellations within 48 hours receive a 50% refund.
Webinar and event registration fees are non-refundable within 24 hours of the event, but can be transferred to another attendee with prior notice.

SECTION 4: REFUND REQUEST PROCESS STEP BY STEP
---

Step 1: Log into your account and navigate to Billing > Invoice History to locate the invoice for which you are requesting a refund.
Step 2: Click on the invoice to open the invoice detail view and locate the "Request Refund" button.
Step 3: Select the reason for the refund from the provided dropdown (Unsatisfied with Service, Technical Issue, Accidental Purchase, No Longer Needed, or Other).
Step 4: For "Other" or "Technical Issue" reasons, a free-text explanation field appears; fill in the details to support your request.
Step 5: Submit the refund request. A confirmation email with a reference number is sent immediately.
Alternatively, customers can submit refund requests by emailing billing@company.com with the invoice number and refund reason.
Telephone refund requests are not accepted to ensure proper documentation and avoid errors.
Live chat support can assist with refund inquiries but cannot process refunds directly; chat agents will submit a refund ticket on your behalf.
Refund tickets are reviewed within 2 business days. The customer is notified of the decision by email.
If the refund is approved, a second email is sent confirming the refund amount and the expected date of return to the original payment method.
If the refund is denied, the email explains the reason for denial and, where applicable, available alternatives such as account credits or plan downgrades.
Refund decisions can be escalated by replying to the denial email and requesting a supervisor review, which is completed within 5 business days.
Customers with a valid consumer protection law right to a refund (such as under EU consumer rights legislation) should state this in their request; such rights are honored irrespective of our standard policy.

SECTION 5: REFUND TIMELINES BY PAYMENT METHOD
---

Credit and debit card refunds are processed within 5-7 business days to the statement of the issuing bank.
The refund first appears in our system as a pending credit within 24-48 hours; the bank may take additional time to post the credit to the cardholder's statement.
Visa and Mastercard typically post credits within 3-5 business days after our processing date.
American Express credits may take up to 7-10 business days to appear on the statement due to their internal processing timelines.
PayPal refunds are instant to the customer's PayPal balance; withdrawal to a bank account from PayPal follows PayPal's own timeline (typically 3-5 business days).
Bank transfer and ACH refunds require the customer's banking details (routing number and account number) if not already on file.
ACH refunds are initiated as a credit ACH transfer and typically arrive within 3-5 business days after initiation.
SEPA bank transfer refunds within the EU typically arrive within 1-2 business days after initiation.
UK BACS refunds typically arrive within 3-5 business days.
Wire transfer refunds are only issued if the original payment exceeded $1,000; smaller wire payment refunds are processed via bank transfer.
Wire transfer refunds may take 7-14 business days due to international banking processing requirements.
Intermediary bank fees deducted from wire transfer refunds are the responsibility of the receiving bank and cannot be reimbursed by us.
Store credit refunds (issued as account billing credits) are applied immediately and visible in the Billing > Credits section of the account.
If a refund fails due to a closed or expired payment method, the customer will be contacted to provide updated payment information for reprocessing.
Failed refunds are retried once with the original payment method before the billing team contacts the customer for alternative arrangements.

SECTION 6: PARTIAL REFUNDS AND PRO-RATED ADJUSTMENTS
---

Partial refunds are issued when a customer is eligible for a refund on only a portion of their purchase.
Partial refunds are calculated based on the proportion of the billing period that was unused at the time of the refund request.
For annual plans cancelled after the 30-day full refund window has passed, no partial refund is provided—the account continues until the end of the annual period.
Exceptions to the no-partial-refund policy for annual plans may be made at management discretion in cases of severe and documented service failures.
When a plan is downgraded mid-cycle, a credit note is generated for the difference between the two plan prices for the remaining days in the cycle.
Credits generated from plan downgrades are applied automatically to the next invoice and reduce the amount billed.
If the total billing credit from a downgrade exceeds the next invoice amount, the surplus credit rolls over to subsequent invoices.
Credit balances on closed accounts are refunded to the original payment method if the credit was the result of a billing error or overpayment exceeding $10.
Credit balances below $10 associated with closed accounts are not refunded and are forfeited upon account closure.
Partial refunds for add-on features are calculated based on the unused portion of the current add-on billing month.
Pro-rated calculations are always based on calendar days, not on usage volume or feature access during the billing period.

SECTION 7: REFUNDS FOR TECHNICAL DEFECTS AND SERVICE FAILURES
---

If a technical defect prevents a customer from using a core product feature, they may be entitled to a refund or service credit regardless of the standard refund window.
A technical defect must be documented by our engineering team to qualify for an out-of-window refund; self-reported errors require investigation before a refund is issued.
To report a technical defect for refund consideration, contact support@company.com with steps to reproduce, error messages, screenshots, and account details.
If the defect is confirmed by our engineering team to be a platform-side bug, a full or partial refund is issued at the team's discretion based on the impact of the defect.
Service outages that affect the customer's access to the platform for more than 2 consecutive hours may qualify for an SLA credit.
SLA credits are calculated as a percentage of the monthly plan fee based on defined uptime tiers:
- 99.9% to 99.0% uptime in a calendar month: 5% credit
- 98.9% to 95.0% uptime: 15% credit
- Below 95.0% uptime: 30% credit
SLA credits are not automatic; they must be requested within 30 days of the end of the affected month by submitting a request to sla@company.com.
Enterprise customers with custom SLA terms defined in their MSA receive SLA credits according to their contract rather than the standard tiers above.
Service outages due to scheduled maintenance, customer-side configuration errors, or third-party service failures do not qualify for SLA credits.
Our status page at status.company.com provides real-time and historical uptime data for all platform components.

SECTION 8: RETURN POLICY FOR PHYSICAL PRODUCTS
---

Physical products (hardware, accessories, printed materials) sold through our store are eligible for return within 30 days of delivery.
The delivery date is determined by the carrier's confirmed delivery scan; the 30-day return window begins from that date.
Returns must be initiated by submitting a return request at company.com/returns or by contacting support@company.com.
A Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) number is issued within 2 business days of a return request.
Items returned without an RMA number may be refused at the warehouse and returned to the sender at their expense.
The customer is responsible for return shipping costs unless the return is due to a defect or fulfillment error.
We provide a prepaid return label for defective or incorrectly shipped items; the cost of the prepaid label is covered by us.
Returned items must be in their original packaging with all accessories, manuals, and components included.
Incomplete returns (missing accessories or documentation) are subject to a partial refund or restocking fee.
Upon receipt and inspection of the return, a refund is issued within 5 business days.
Defective products under warranty may be repaired or replaced rather than refunded, depending on the warranty terms and availability of replacement units.
Product warranty details are included with each hardware product shipment and are available on the product's page on our website.

SECTION 9: CHARGEBACKS AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
---

A chargeback is a reversal process initiated by the customer's bank or credit card issuer when the customer disputes a charge.
We encourage customers to contact us directly to resolve billing disputes before initiating a chargeback, as chargebacks carry costs and delays for both parties.
When a chargeback is received, the disputed amount is immediately placed in a temporary hold and the associated account may be suspended pending investigation.
Our billing team has 7-10 business days to respond to a chargeback with evidence to confirm or refute the dispute.
Evidence submitted in chargeback responses includes transaction records, usage logs, communication records, and subscriber agreement acceptance.
If the chargeback is resolved in the customer's favor, the account remains suspended to prevent further losses.
If the chargeback is resolved in our favor, the transaction amount is retained and the account may be reactivated subject to a review.
Customers whose chargebacks are denied by the bank but who have a legitimate billing concern may contact us to pursue an alternative resolution.
Repeated chargebacks by the same account can result in permanent termination and blacklisting of payment methods per our fraud prevention policies.
Friendly fraud (intentional chargebacks on legitimate transactions) is actively contested and may be referred to the appropriate authorities where applicable.

SECTION 10: REFUNDS FOR ENTERPRISE AND VOLUME CUSTOMERS
---

Enterprise refund policies are defined in the individual customer's Master Service Agreement (MSA) and may differ from the standard policy described above.
Enterprise MSAs may include custom refund windows, SLA-triggered credit schedules, and specific escalation paths for financial disputes.
Enterprise customers should refer to their MSA terms and contact their dedicated account manager for refund and credit requests.
Volume license customers who reduce their seat count mid-contract are subject to the terms outlined in their volume license agreement.
Seat count reductions are generally not refunded during an active contract period; reductions take effect at the next contract renewal.
Enterprise annual contracts that are terminated early are subject to early termination fees as defined in the contract; unused days are not refunded beyond any early termination credit clause.
Enterprise customers migrating between platforms (off-boarding transitions) may be eligible for a pro-rated exit credit as a goodwill gesture, subject to management approval.
Billing disputes for enterprise accounts are escalated to the Senior Account Manager and the VP of Customer Success for resolution within 5 business days.
Enterprise customers have a dedicated billing inbox at enterprise_billing@company.com staffed by Senior Billing Specialists.
Quarterly billing reviews are offered to enterprise customers to ensure billing accuracy and address any discrepancies proactively before they become disputes.
