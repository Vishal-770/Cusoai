ACCOUNT SUSPENSION AND SECURITY POLICY

---
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS — ACCOUNT SECURITY & SUSPENSION
---

Q: My account was suspended. Why?
A: Accounts are suspended for: Terms of Service violations, suspected fraud, non-payment, or repeated refund abuse. A suspension notice is sent to your registered email explaining the reason and how to appeal.

Q: How do I appeal a suspension?
A: Email appeals@company.com within 14 days of suspension with your account details and a written explanation. Appeals must come from your registered email address.

Q: My account was suspended by mistake. What do I do?
A: Email appeals@company.com immediately. Do NOT create a new account — that is considered circumvention and may result in permanent termination. If the suspension was in error, your account will be reactivated within 2 hours of appeal approval with service credit compensation.

Q: How long does an appeal take?
A: Our Trust and Safety team reviews suspension cases within 48 hours of the initial report. Supervisor escalations take up to 5 business days.

Q: I think my account was hacked. What should I do?
A: Immediately: (1) change your password, (2) go to Account Settings > Security > Active Sessions and revoke all sessions, (3) email security@company.com with details. Our security team responds within 1 hour during business hours (9am–6pm Mon–Fri).

Q: Someone logged into my account from a different location.
A: Revoke all sessions from Account Settings > Security > Active Sessions. Change your password immediately and enable 2FA if not already active. Report to security@company.com.

Q: How do I secure my account?
A: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (Settings > Security > 2FA). Use a unique, strong password (12+ characters). Review active sessions regularly. Never share login credentials.

Q: Will I lose my data if my account is suspended?
A: Temporary and full suspensions do not delete data. If an account remains unpaid for 30+ days post-suspension and is deactivated, data is retained for 14 more days. After that window, data is permanently deleted.

Q: I received an email saying my password was reset but I didn't request it.
A: Someone may be attempting to access your account. Click the "I did not request this reset" link in the email — this locks your account immediately. Then contact security@company.com.

Q: I found a security vulnerability in your platform. What do I do?
A: Submit it through our secure portal at security.company.com/disclose with full reproduction steps. We acknowledge within 24 hours and provide a fix timeline within 72 hours. Our Bug Bounty Program offers rewards from $100–$10,000 based on severity.

Q: I received a phishing email claiming to be from you. What do I do?
A: Do not click any links. Forward the email to security@company.com. We will investigate and warn other users if the phishing campaign is confirmed.

Q: Can a support agent see my password?
A: Never. Passwords are stored as bcrypt hashes and cannot be read by anyone, including our engineers. Agents can only initiate a forced password reset — they cannot view or set your password directly.

Q: How do I delete my account and data?
A: Go to Account Settings > Account > Delete Account, or email privacy@company.com with a deletion request. Data is fully deleted within 30 days of account closure per our data retention policy.

Q: My subscription is cancelled. When is my data deleted?
A: Data is preserved for 30 days after cancellation to allow reactivation. After 30 days with no reactivation, data deletion begins.

Q: I was charged during my suspension. Can I get a refund?
A: Recurring payments are paused during suspension — you should not be charged. If a charge did occur during suspension, contact billing@company.com with the invoice number for immediate investigation and refund.

---


A suspension notice is sent to the registered email address with the specific reason and any steps to appeal the decision.
To appeal a suspension, the customer must email appeals@company.com with their account details and a written explanation within 14 days.
Accounts suspended for non-payment are automatically reactivated within 2 hours of successful payment processing.

Our security team acknowledges reports within 1 hour during business hours (9am-6pm Mon-Fri).

SECTION 2: DETAILED ACCOUNT SUSPENSION PROCEDURES
---

Account suspension is a protective measure applied when activities on an account violate our Terms of Service, endanger other users, or create financial risk for the platform.
There are three categories of account suspension: temporary suspension, full suspension, and permanent termination.
A temporary suspension lasts between 24 hours and 7 days and is applied for minor or first-time violations.
A full suspension prevents all account access and is applied when violations are confirmed and require investigation.
Permanent termination is irreversible and is reserved for fraud, criminal activity, or severe repeated violations.
When an account is suspended, all active sessions are immediately terminated and all API tokens associated with the account are revoked.
Scheduled recurring payments are paused during suspension and will not be charged until the account is reactivated.
Any active data exports or pending file downloads at the time of suspension will be cancelled immediately.
Sub-accounts linked to a suspended primary account are also suspended as a precautionary measure.
The suspension notification email includes a case reference number that must be quoted in all correspondence related to the appeal.
Our Trust and Safety team reviews each suspension case within 48 hours of the initial report that triggered it.
If an account is suspended due to suspected fraudulent payment, all outstanding orders associated with the account are placed on hold pending verification.
Accounts detected sharing login credentials with multiple users are flagged for a Terms of Service review prior to suspension.
Automated systems monitor for unusual login patterns, bulk data downloads, and account activity that deviates significantly from historical behavior.
When automated detection flags an account, a human reviewer must confirm the violation before suspension is applied in all non-emergency cases.
Emergency suspensions triggered by confirmed fraud or active security breaches may be applied immediately without prior human review.
Customers who believe they have been suspended in error should not attempt to create a new account; this is considered circumvention and will result in permanent termination.
The appeals process involves submitting a written explanation, supporting evidence, and agreeing to an identity verification check.
Appeals submitted by email must come from the registered email address on file; appeals from other email addresses will not be processed.
If an appeal is denied, the customer receives a detailed explanation of the evidence supporting the suspension decision.
A second appeal can be submitted only if new evidence is presented; repeat appeals without new evidence are automatically declined.
In cases where suspension was incorrectly applied, the account is reactivated within 2 hours of the appeal approval, and any lost service time is compensated with account credits.
Account credits issued as compensation for erroneous suspension are calculated based on the daily rate of the customer's subscription plan.
Customers undergoing an active suspension cannot access support chat but may contact us by email at appeals@company.com.

SECTION 3: SECURITY BREACH AND INCIDENT RESPONSE
---

A security breach is defined as any unauthorized access to, disclosure of, or destruction of customer data or system assets.
Our incident response team operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week to respond to reported security events.
All employees are required to complete security incident response training annually and demonstrated proficiency assessments semi-annually.
When a potential breach is detected, the incident is assigned a severity level between P0 (critical) and P3 (low) based on the scope and nature of the exposure.
A P0 breach involves exposure of sensitive personal data, financial information, or credentials affecting more than 1,000 users and requires immediate containment within 1 hour.
A P1 breach involves targeted unauthorized access to specific accounts or limited system components and requires response within 4 hours.
A P2 breach involves unauthorized read-only access to non-sensitive data and requires response within 24 hours.
A P3 breach involves attempted but unsuccessful intrusion attempts or minor system anomalies and requires response within 72 hours.
Upon detecting a P0 or P1 security incident, affected accounts are automatically locked pending a password reset by the account holder.
Affected customers are notified of a breach via the registered email address within 72 hours of the breach being confirmed, in compliance with GDPR Article 33 and state breach notification laws.
Breach notification emails include the nature of the data exposed, the likely source of the breach, steps taken to contain it, and recommended actions for the customer.
Our legal and compliance teams evaluate breach notification obligations under applicable laws in all jurisdictions where affected customers reside.
All security incidents are documented in a private incident log that is reviewed monthly by our Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).
Third-party security audits are conducted quarterly to assess and improve our incident detection and response capabilities.
Penetration testing is performed by certified external security firms at least twice per year and after any major platform architecture changes.
Upon confirming a data breach, a dedicated incident hotline is activated for affected customers at breach-support@company.com.
Customers participating in our Bug Bounty Program who discover and responsibly disclose security vulnerabilities are eligible for rewards between $100 and $10,000 depending on severity.
All vulnerability disclosures must be submitted through our secure portal at security.company.com/disclose with full reproduction steps.
Submitted vulnerability reports are triaged within 24 hours and a fix timeline is communicated to the reporter within 72 hours.
We commit to not pursuing legal action against security researchers who discover and disclose vulnerabilities in good faith per our responsible disclosure policy.
Post-incident, a root cause analysis report is prepared within 14 days and made available to enterprise customers upon request.

SECTION 4: PASSWORD AND AUTHENTICATION POLICIES
---

Passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
Common passwords, dictionary words, and passwords that have appeared in known data breaches are rejected by our password validation system.
Password strength is evaluated in real time during creation and customers are guided toward stronger choices before submission is accepted.
Users are required to change their password if it matches a password found in any publicly known data breach database.
Passwords are stored using bcrypt hashing with a minimum work factor of 12; plaintext passwords are never stored or transmitted.
Password history is enforced: customers cannot reuse any of their last 10 passwords.
Temporary passwords issued by support agents expire after 24 hours and must be changed on first login.
Password reset links are cryptographically signed, unique per request, and expire after 15 minutes for security.
If a password reset link expires before use, the customer must initiate a new reset request; expired links cannot be extended.
Support agents cannot view, reveal, or set permanent passwords for customer accounts; they can only initiate a forced password reset flow.
Enterprise accounts can enforce custom password policies including minimum length, complexity, rotation intervals, and lockout thresholds through the Admin panel.
Shared account logins are prohibited; each user must have a unique login credential tied to their individual identity.
Service account credentials used for API integrations must be rotated at least every 90 days and must not be shared across multiple services.
Passphrase-style passwords (long phrases without special characters) are accepted provided they meet the minimum entropy requirements.
Customers are encouraged to use a reputable password manager to generate and store unique passwords for each service they use.
Our systems log failed password attempts, rate-limit authentication requests, and trigger alerts for patterns consistent with credential stuffing attacks.
After 5 consecutive failed login attempts within a 10-minute window, the account is locked for 30 minutes automatically.
After 10 consecutive failed attempts across multiple windows, the account is locked indefinitely and requires manual verification to unlock.
Password reset via SMS fallback is available for accounts with a verified phone number but is considered less secure than email-based reset.
Biometric authentication (fingerprint, face ID) is supported on mobile applications as a convenience login method in addition to standard credentials.

SECTION 5: TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (2FA) DETAILED POLICY
---

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of verification beyond a password, significantly reducing the risk of unauthorized account access.
We support the following 2FA methods: authenticator apps (TOTP), SMS one-time codes, hardware security keys (FIDO2/WebAuthn), and email-based OTPs.
Authenticator apps such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, and Authy are the recommended 2FA method due to their resilience against SIM swap attacks.
To set up TOTP-based 2FA, navigate to Account Settings > Security > Two-Factor Authentication and scan the displayed QR code with your authenticator app.
After scanning, enter the 6-digit code to verify the setup is working before activation is confirmed.
Upon enabling 2FA, the system generates 10 single-use backup codes that can be used if the primary 2FA method is unavailable.
Backup codes must be stored securely; each code can only be used once and they are invalidated if 2FA is disabled and re-enabled.
SMS-based 2FA sends a one-time code to the verified mobile number associated with the account; codes expire after 10 minutes.
SMS-based 2FA is susceptible to SIM swap attacks and is therefore not recommended for accounts with access to sensitive data.
Hardware security keys using the FIDO2/WebAuthn standard provide the highest level of 2FA protection and are phishing-resistant.
Up to 5 hardware security keys can be registered per account, allowing for multiple backup keys.
Enterprise administrators can mandate 2FA for all users under their organization and can specify which 2FA methods are permitted.
If a user loses access to their 2FA method, they must contact support and complete an enhanced identity verification process to regain access.
Identity verification for 2FA recovery requires submitting a government-issued photo ID and confirmation from the registered email address.
2FA recovery typically takes 2-3 business days to process to prevent social engineering attacks.
Accounts with 2FA enabled receive an email notification each time a new device logs in successfully.
Disabling 2FA on an account generates an immediate alert email and requires re-confirmation with the existing 2FA method before proceeding.
Enterprise organizations can enable conditional access rules that require 2FA only from unrecognized networks or geographic locations outside the organization's defined perimeter.
Our system supports 2FA enforcement at the role level, allowing organizations to require stronger authentication only for administrator or privileged roles.
All 2FA configuration changes are logged and auditable by organization administrators in the Security Audit Log.

SECTION 6: ACCOUNT RECOVERY PROCEDURES
---

Account recovery is the process of regaining access to an account when standard login methods are unavailable.
The first step in account recovery is always attempting the standard password reset via the registered email address.
If the registered email is no longer accessible, the customer must contact our support team and provide identity verification documents.
Accepted identity verification documents include government-issued photo IDs such as passports, national ID cards, or driver's licenses.
The name on the identity document must match the name registered on the account; discrepancies require additional verification steps.
For business accounts, recovery may require verification from an authorized representative and submission of business registration documents.
Account recovery without access to the registered email typically takes 3-5 business days due to the enhanced verification requirements.
We do not offer expedited account recovery without charge; standard timelines apply regardless of urgency claimed.
If a customer suspects their registered email has also been compromised, they should report this and our security team will assist in securing both accounts simultaneously.
After successful identity verification, a new verified email address is set on the account and a password reset is initiated to that new address.
Any active 2FA methods on the account are suspended during recovery and must be re-enrolled after access is restored.
All account recovery attempts are logged with timestamps, IP addresses, and verification methods used for security audit purposes.
Customers who have previously set up account recovery security questions can use these as a supplementary verification method, though this alone is not sufficient for email change requests.
Security questions must be answered character-for-character as originally set; case-insensitive matching is applied.
Accounts that have been inactive for more than 2 years require additional ownership verification before recovery is processed.
After account recovery is complete, the customer receives a full summary of security changes made during the process.
Our support team will never ask for a customer's full password under any circumstances during the recovery process.
If you receive a call or message claiming to be from our support team asking for your full password, this is a phishing attempt; report it to security@company.com immediately.

SECTION 7: SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY MONITORING AND ALERTS
---

Our platform employs a real-time threat detection system that continuously analyzes account activity for anomalous patterns.
Signals monitored include login location, login time, device fingerprint, API usage volume, data export volume, and navigation patterns.
When anomalous activity is detected, the system assigns a risk score to the session. Sessions above the threshold trigger a step-up authentication challenge.
Step-up authentication requires the user to re-verify their identity via 2FA or email OTP before continuing the flagged action.
If step-up authentication fails or is not completed within 5 minutes, the session is terminated and the account is flagged for review.
Customers receive an email alert any time a new device or browser is used to log into their account successfully.
Login alerts include the device type, approximate geographic location derived from IP address, and timestamp of the login event.
If the customer did not perform the login, the alert email contains a one-click "Secure My Account" button that immediately revokes all sessions and triggers a password reset.
Simultaneous logins from geographically distant locations within a short timeframe (impossible travel) trigger an automatic risk flag.
Bulk data download exceeding 500 MB within a 1-hour period flags the account for an automated security review.
Repeated API calls with authentication errors within a short window trigger rate limiting and temporary IP blocking for that endpoint.
Customers can review their full login history, including successful and failed attempts, from Account Settings > Security > Login History.
Login history is retained and viewable for the past 90 days.
Customers can set custom security alerts through Account Settings > Security > Alerts, specifying which event types should trigger notifications.
Notification channels for security alerts include email, SMS, push notifications (mobile app), and webhook integrations.
Enterprise customers can pipe security alert events to their SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) systems via our security webhook API.
The security webhook payload includes event type, timestamp, account ID, IP address, device information, and a severity level classification.
Our threat intelligence team subscribes to multiple cybersecurity feeds and proactively blocks IP ranges associated with known malicious actors.
Geo-blocking can be configured by enterprise administrators to deny login attempts from specific countries or regions.
IP allowlisting for enterprise accounts ensures that only connections from approved IP addresses or CIDR ranges can access the account.

SECTION 8: DATA PRIVACY AND ACCESS CONTROL
---

We are committed to processing personal data in accordance with GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy regulations.
Customers have the right to access, correct, port, and request deletion of their personal data at any time.
Data access requests are processed within 30 days; complex requests may take up to 60 days with advance notice to the customer.
Personal data exported in response to a data access request is provided in a machine-readable format (JSON or CSV).
Account deletion requests result in permanent removal of personal data from our production systems within 30 days.
Backups containing personal data are purged within 90 days following an account deletion request, in accordance with our data retention schedule.
We do not sell personal data to third parties under any circumstances.
Data shared with third-party service providers is governed by Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) that enforce equivalent privacy standards.
A current list of third-party sub-processors is maintained and publicly accessible at company.com/sub-processors.
Customers are notified at least 30 days in advance of any new sub-processor being added that will process their personal data.
Access to customer data within our organization is governed by role-based access control (RBAC) with least-privilege principles.
Customer support agents can access the minimum data necessary to resolve support tickets; they cannot access payment card details or sensitive credentials.
All internal access to customer data is logged and subject to regular access review audits.
Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 encryption. Data in transit is protected by TLS 1.2 or higher.
Database encryption keys are managed by a dedicated key management service with automated rotation every 12 months.
Our data centers are SOC 2 Type II certified and undergo annual third-party security audits.
Customers can appoint a Data Protection Officer contact in their enterprise account settings for all regulatory correspondence.

SECTION 9: SESSION MANAGEMENT AND DEVICE TRUST
---

User sessions are managed using short-lived cryptographically signed JWT tokens with a 1-hour expiry.
Refresh tokens with a 30-day rolling window are used to silently re-authenticate active sessions without requiring re-login.
A user can have a maximum of 10 concurrent active sessions across different devices.
All active sessions can be viewed and individually revoked from Account Settings > Security > Active Sessions.
The "Sign Out All Devices" option in Account Settings immediately invalidates all refresh tokens and terminates all active sessions globally.
Trusted device recognition uses a combination of device fingerprinting and a persistent cookie to identify returning trusted devices.
A device is marked as trusted after successful login plus confirmed 2FA verification on that device.
The trust status of a device persists for 90 days; after 90 days, 2FA re-verification is required from that device.
Customers can view, name, and revoke trusted devices from Account Settings > Security > Trusted Devices.
Sessions are automatically terminated after 30 minutes of inactivity; users are warned at the 25-minute mark.
Enterprise administrators can configure shorter session timeout windows (minimum 5 minutes) for higher-security environments.
The mobile app maintains a longer background session (7 days) but requires biometric or PIN re-verification whenever the app returns to the foreground after 15 minutes of inactivity.
Our API uses OAuth 2.0 with scoped access tokens; tokens can be granted granular permissions that limit the operations they can perform.
API tokens do not expire by default but can be set to expire on a schedule through the developer dashboard.
Rotating API tokens is strongly recommended; our API supports zero-downtime token rotation by accepting a brief dual-validity window.
All API token creation, usage, and revocation events are included in the security audit log.
Service accounts can be created for automated processes; service accounts do not have UI access and are limited to declared API permission scopes.

SECTION 10: ENTERPRISE ACCOUNT SECURITY FEATURES
---

Enterprise accounts have access to an advanced security dashboard providing a consolidated view of organization-wide security posture.
The security dashboard displays metrics including active session count, failed login attempts, 2FA adoption rate, and recent security events.
Single Sign-On (SSO) via SAML 2.0 is available for enterprise accounts, allowing integration with identity providers such as Okta, Azure AD, OneLogin, and PingIdentity.
When SSO is enabled, username/password login is disabled for all organization users; all authentication is routed through the configured identity provider.
Enterprise SSO setup requires a Domain Verification step where the customer must add a DNS TXT record to prove ownership of their email domain.
SCIM (System for Cross-domain Identity Management) provisioning is supported for automatic user creation, suspension, and deactivation based on the identity provider's directory.
SCIM provisioning ensures that when an employee leaves the organization and is deactivated in the IdP, their platform account is automatically suspended within 15 minutes.
Organization-wide security policies can be enforced via the Enterprise Admin Panel, including password strength requirements, 2FA mandates, and allowed login methods.
Audit logs for enterprise accounts capture all user actions, administrative changes, and security events with immutable timestamps and user attribution.
Audit logs can be exported in CSV or JSON format and can be streamed to external log management platforms (Splunk, Datadog, ELK) via secure webhook.
Enterprise accounts can define a Security Contact who receives all security-related notifications and alerts for the organization.
IP restriction policies at the organization level block users from logging in outside approved networks, providing an additional access control layer.
Enterprise accounts include a dedicated Customer Security Liaison from our security team who is available for quarterly security reviews.
Vulnerability scan reports and security questionnaire completion support are available to enterprise customers for compliance and vendor risk management purposes.
Enterprise accounts can opt in to our Advanced Threat Protection add-on, which provides enhanced behavioral analytics and automated threat response capabilities.
