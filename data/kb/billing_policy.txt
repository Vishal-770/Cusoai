BILLING AND PAYMENT POLICY

---
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS — BILLING
---

Q: How do I cancel my subscription?
A: Go to Account Settings > Billing > Subscription and click "Cancel Subscription". Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period. Your data is preserved for 30 days after cancellation.

Q: How do I update or change my credit card?
A: Go to Account Settings > Billing > Payment Methods and click "Add Payment Method". Set it as primary, then you can remove the old card.

Q: Why was I charged more than I expected?
A: Common reasons: a plan upgrade was pro-rated and charged immediately, an annual renewal occurred, or an add-on was activated. Check Billing > Invoice History for a detailed breakdown of charges.

Q: I never received my invoice. How do I get it?
A: Invoices are in Billing > Invoice History and can be downloaded as PDF. Check your billing email address in Billing Settings — it may differ from your login email. Also check your spam folder for emails from billing@company.com.

Q: My payment failed. What happens to my account?
A: We retry automatically 3 times over 7 days (day 1, day 3, day 7). After all retries fail, your account enters a 3-day grace period, then suspends if unpaid. Updating your payment method and retrying will immediately restore access.

Q: My account was suspended for non-payment. How do I reactivate it?
A: Update your payment method in Billing Settings and make a payment. Your account reactivates within 2 hours of successful payment. No data is lost during suspension.

Q: Can I get an invoice with my company name and VAT number?
A: Yes. Add your company name, billing address, and VAT/tax ID in Billing > Billing Settings. All future invoices will include this information. You can also edit existing invoices for enterprise accounts.

Q: How does pro-rated billing work when I upgrade?
A: You are charged immediately for the difference in plan price multiplied by the remaining days in your current billing cycle. Formula: (New plan daily rate − old plan daily rate) × remaining days = charge.

Q: When does my subscription renew?
A: Monthly plans renew on the same day each month as your signup date. Annual plans renew on the anniversary of signup. You receive an invoice notification 3 days before renewal.

Q: How do I switch from monthly to annual billing?
A: Go to Account Settings > Billing > Subscription > Change Plan and select the annual option. You'll be charged the annual price immediately and your billing cycle resets to that date.

Q: Can I pause my subscription instead of cancelling?
A: We do not currently offer a pause feature. You can cancel and reactivate within 30 days without losing data.

Q: I see an unauthorized charge on my statement. What do I do?
A: Report unauthorized charges to billing@company.com within 60 days. Include the charge amount, date, and your account email. We will investigate and issue a refund if the charge was not authorised.

Q: Do you charge VAT or sales tax?
A: Tax is applied based on your billing address and applicable local regulations. VAT/tax amounts are shown on each invoice.

Q: What payment methods do you accept?
A: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, Diners Club, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay. ACH (US), SEPA (EU), and BACS (UK) bank transfers are available for enterprise annual plans.

Q: Can I get Net 30 payment terms?
A: Net 30 is available for enterprise customers with approved credit. Contact sales@company.com to request a credit review.

Q: How do I get a receipt for my purchase?
A: Every invoice in Billing > Invoice History can be downloaded as a PDF receipt. A receipt is also emailed to your billing address automatically after each successful payment.

SECTION 1: STANDARD BILLING OVERVIEW
---


Invoices are generated automatically at the start of each billing cycle and sent to the registered email.
If a payment fails, we retry it automatically 3 times over the next 3 days before suspending the account.
Customers can update their payment method at any time from the Billing section of their Account settings.
All transactions are encrypted using industry-standard TLS/SSL protocols.
Disputes and unauthorized charges should be reported to our billing department within 60 days.
Subscription plan upgrades are billed immediately and pro-rated for the remaining days in the billing cycle.
Downgrades take effect at the end of the current billing cycle and are not pro-rated.

SECTION 2: DETAILED PAYMENT METHODS AND PROCESSING
---

We accept Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, and Diners Club credit and debit cards.
PayPal is supported as both a one-time payment method and as a recurring billing source for subscription accounts.
Bank transfers (ACH in the US, SEPA in Europe, BACS in the UK) are accepted for annual enterprise plans with a minimum contract value of $1,000.
Wire transfers are accepted for enterprise contracts above $10,000 annually; a $25 wire processing fee applies per transfer.
Cryptocurrency payments are not currently accepted on any plan tier.
Apple Pay and Google Pay are supported for checkout on mobile browsers and within the native mobile applications.
SEPA Direct Debit mandates are created digitally and comply with SEPA Core Direct Debit Scheme requirements.
ACH pull payments are processed through Nacha-compliant banking rails with standard 3-5 business day settlement times.
When adding a new payment method, a temporary $1.00 authorization hold is placed to verify the card; this hold is released within 3-5 business days.
Card verification uses 3D Secure (3DS2) authentication for transactions flagged as potentially high risk, adding an additional authentication step.
We do not store raw card numbers; all card data is tokenized by our PCI-DSS Level 1 certified payment processor.
Our payment infrastructure is certified PCI-DSS Level 1, the highest level of payment security certification available.
Customers can store multiple payment methods on their account and designate one as the primary billing method.
The primary payment method is charged for all subscription renewals and automatic payments.
Secondary payment methods serve as fallback if the primary payment method fails.
To remove a payment method that is currently set as primary, the customer must first designate another method as primary.
A payment method cannot be deleted if there are any pending or outstanding invoice payments that have not yet been cleared.

SECTION 3: INVOICE AND BILLING CYCLE MANAGEMENT
---

Billing cycles are either monthly or annual depending on the subscription plan selected at signup.
Monthly billing cycles renew on the same day each month as the original signup date.
If the renewal date falls on a day that does not exist in a given month (e.g., the 31st in February), billing occurs on the last day of that month.
Annual billing cycles renew on the anniversary of the original signup date each year.
Invoices are generated 3 days before the billing cycle renews to give customers advance notice.
Invoice notifications are sent to the primary billing email address, which can differ from the account login email and is configured in Billing Settings.
Multiple billing email recipients can be configured for enterprise accounts to ensure finance teams receive invoices directly.
Invoices are available for download in PDF format from the Billing > Invoice History section of the account dashboard.
Invoice PDFs include a full breakdown of charges, applied discounts, applicable taxes, and payment method used.
Invoices are retained in the system for 7 years for accounting and legal compliance purposes.
Custom purchase order (PO) numbers can be added to invoices for enterprise customers; the PO number will appear on all invoices for the billing period specified.
Custom billing addresses, including company name, VAT/tax identification numbers, and postal address, can be set in Billing Settings for accurate invoice formatting.
Net 30 payment terms are available for enterprise customers with approved credit; post-payment invoicing is subject to credit review and approval.
Late payment on Net 30 invoices will incur a late fee of 1.5% of the outstanding balance per month after the due date.
Consolidated billing allows enterprise parent accounts to aggregate charges across multiple sub-accounts into a single monthly invoice.
Billing timezone is UTC by default; enterprise customers can configure their preferred billing timezone in account settings.

SECTION 4: PAYMENT FAILURE AND DUNNING PROCESS
---

When a payment fails, our dunning management system initiates an automated retry and notification sequence.
The first retry attempt occurs 1 day after the initial payment failure.
The second retry attempt occurs 3 days after the initial payment failure.
The third and final retry attempt occurs 7 days after the initial payment failure.
Between retry attempts, the customer receives email notifications at each failed attempt with instructions to update their payment method.
If all three retry attempts fail, the subscription enters a 3-day grace period during which the account remains active but is flagged.
During the grace period, the customer can update their payment method and trigger a manual payment attempt to restore normal billing.
If the subscription is not resolved by the end of the grace period, the account is suspended for non-payment.
Suspended accounts are fully reactivated within 2 hours of successful payment processing.
No subscription data or settings are lost during a non-payment suspension period.
If an account remains unpaid for 30 days post-suspension, it is scheduled for deactivation and the customer is notified 7 days in advance.
After deactivation for non-payment, data is retained for 14 days during which reactivation with full payment of outstanding balance is possible.
After the 14-day post-deactivation retention window, data is permanently deleted and cannot be recovered.
Enterprise customers with Net 30 terms do not enter automated dunning; instead, account managers follow up directly on overdue invoices.
Customers can opt in to receiving payment failure SMS alerts in addition to the standard email notifications through Billing Settings.
The reason for payment failure (insufficient funds, card expired, do not honor, etc.) is communicated via email where available from the payment processor.
Updating an expired card number and expiration date on the same primary card requires re-entering the card details, not just the new expiration date.

SECTION 5: SUBSCRIPTION PLANS AND UPGRADES
---

We offer Individual, Team, Business, and Enterprise subscription tiers with increasing feature access and user limits.
The Individual plan supports 1 user seat and is billed monthly or annually.
The Team plan supports between 2 and 25 user seats and includes collaborative features.
The Business plan supports between 26 and 250 user seats and includes advanced administrative controls.
The Enterprise plan is for organizations above 250 seats and includes custom SLAs, dedicated support, SSO, and custom contracts.
Plan pricing is published on the company.com/pricing page and is subject to change with 30 days' advance notice to active subscribers.
Existing subscribers are grandfathered at their current plan pricing until they choose to change plans or the end of their current annual term.
Upgrading from a lower tier plan to a higher tier plan takes effect immediately.
When upgrading mid-cycle, the charge for the upgraded plan is pro-rated based on the number of days remaining in the current billing cycle.
Proration calculation: (Daily rate of new plan × remaining days) minus (Daily rate of old plan × remaining days) = amount charged immediately.
If a credit results from a downgrade proration calculation (where allowed), it is applied to the account as a billing credit toward future charges.
Downgrading from a higher tier to a lower tier takes effect at the end of the current billing cycle, not immediately.
If the current user seat count exceeds the limit of the downgraded plan, the downgrade cannot be completed until the user count is reduced.
Annual plan discounts are published on the pricing page; annual subscribers typically receive 2 months of subscription free compared to monthly billing.
Volume discounts for large user seat counts on the Business and Enterprise tiers are negotiated individually with our sales team.
Add-on features (advanced analytics, additional storage, priority support) can be added to any plan at any time and are billed monthly.
Add-ons billed monthly are not discounted when included with annual plans unless specifically negotiated.
Trial plans offer 14 days of full Business plan functionality with no payment information required.
After a free trial expires, the account downgrades to a read-only Free tier; data is preserved for 30 days for conversion to a paid plan.

SECTION 6: TAXATION AND COMPLIANCE
---

We are required by law to collect sales tax, VAT, or GST in jurisdictions where we have a tax nexus or are otherwise required to collect tax.
US customers may be charged state and/or local sales tax depending on the tax laws in their state of residence.
EU customers are charged VAT at the applicable rate for their country of residence in accordance with EU VAT rules.
UK customers are charged 20% VAT in accordance with HMRC requirements.
Australian customers are charged 10% GST in accordance with the Australian Tax Office requirements.
Canadian customers may be charged GST, HST, or PST depending on their province.
Business customers in VAT-obligated jurisdictions can provide their VAT ID in Billing Settings to apply VAT reverse charge mechanisms where applicable.
A valid VAT ID will be validated against government databases and, if confirmed, zero-rated VAT will be applied on future invoices.
Tax-exempt organizations (501(c)(3) in the US, or equivalent abroad) must submit a valid exemption certificate to billing@company.com to have taxes removed from their account.
Tax exemption review is completed within 5 business days and, once approved, applies retroactively to invoices within the current calendar month.
Taxes are calculated at the point of invoice generation and are based on the billing address on file; it is the customer's responsibility to keep the billing address accurate.
We do not provide tax advice; customers should consult a qualified tax professional for guidance on deductibility or VAT reclaim.

SECTION 7: BILLING DISPUTES AND CHARGE CORRECTIONS
---

A billing dispute should be filed within 60 days of the charge appearing on the invoice.
To file a billing dispute, contact billing@company.com with the invoice number, amount disputed, and a description of why the charge is incorrect.
Billing disputes are acknowledged within 2 business days.
While a billing dispute is under review, the disputed amount will not be sent to collections.
If a dispute is resolved in the customer's favor, a credit note is issued and applied against the next invoice or refunded to the original payment method.
If a dispute is resolved against the customer, a detailed explanation is provided along with supporting evidence of the charge legitimacy.
Customers who initiate a credit card chargeback without first filing a billing dispute may have their account suspended pending resolution.
We encourage all billing concerns to be resolved directly before escalating to a chargeback, as chargebacks carry administrative costs for both parties.
A formal dispute resolution process, including escalation to senior billing management, is available if initial dispute resolution is not satisfactory.
All billing dispute communications are tracked in our CRM and are available to the customer upon request.

SECTION 8: BILLING CREDITS AND PROMOTIONS
---

Billing credits are applied automatically to the next invoice and reduce the amount due.
Billing credits do not expire and roll over to subsequent invoices until fully consumed.
Credits cannot be converted to cash refunds unless the account is being closed and the credit is the result of a billing error or overpayment.
Referral credits are issued within 7 days of the referred customer completing their first payment.
Promotional codes can be applied at checkout or in Billing Settings; they are single-use and cannot be combined with other promotion codes unless explicitly stated.
Promotional discounts are applied at the plan level and are reflected on all invoices during the promotion period.
Promotional pricing periods renew at standard pricing unless a recurring discount arrangement was explicitly agreed in writing.
Billing credits issued as SLA compensation for service downtime are calculated based on the affected plan's monthly or daily rate and the duration of the outage.
Enterprise SLA credit entitlements are defined in the individual customer's Master Service Agreement and may differ from standard credits described here.

SECTION 9: ANNUAL BILLING AND MULTI-YEAR CONTRACTS
---

Annual billing requires payment of the full year's subscription cost upfront.
Annual plans are not refundable after the first 30 days except where mandated by applicable consumer protection laws.
Within the first 30 days of an annual subscription, customers may request cancellation and receive a pro-rated refund for the unused months.
Multi-year contract pricing is available for Enterprise customers and involves custom negotiation with the sales team.
Multi-year contracts are locked at the agreed pricing for the contract duration regardless of standard pricing changes.
Early termination of a multi-year contract is subject to an early termination fee defined in the contract, typically 25% of the remaining contract value.
Custom payment schedules (quarterly, bi-annually) for multi-year enterprise contracts can be arranged with the finance team.
Annual plan renewals generate a renewal reminder email 60 days before the renewal date to allow customers time to review and adjust their subscription.
A second renewal reminder is sent 14 days before the renewal date.
Manual annual renewals that require a new purchase order must be submitted at least 5 business days before the renewal date to avoid service interruption.

SECTION 10: INTERNATIONAL BILLING AND CURRENCY
---

Billing is conducted in USD (US Dollars) by default for all global customers.
Enterprise customers may request billing in EUR, GBP, AUD, or CAD; currency lock-in must be confirmed in writing before the contract start.
Exchange rate conversion for non-USD billing is fixed at contract signing and does not fluctuate with market exchange rates during the contract period.
International wire transfer customers are responsible for any intermediary bank fees; our stated price is the net amount we must receive.
Billing addresses and payment methods can be based in any country; we do not restrict payment processing based on country of origin except where prohibited by international sanctions.
We comply with OFAC sanctions lists and do not process payments from individuals or entities on restricted lists.
Currency conversion on the customer's credit card statement is handled by the issuing bank; our charge is always in the agreed billing currency.
Multi-currency invoice display is available for enterprise accounts, showing both the billing currency and a converted local currency estimate for informational purposes.
